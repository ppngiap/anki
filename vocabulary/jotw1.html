Jean Craighead George Julie of the Wolves

Part I

Vocabulary.com
In this classic novel, an Eskimo girl lost in the arctic wilderness must fight for survival while she grapples with her sense of identity. Learn these words from the Newbery Medal winner by Jean Craighead George.

Explore the Words

definitions & notes only words
  1. discern
    detect with the senses
    She had been watching the wolves for two days, trying to discern which of their sounds and movements expressed goodwill and friendship.
  2. bleak
    unpleasantly cold and damp
    The length of her limbs and the beauty of her face were of no use to Miyax as she lay on the lichen-speckled frost heave in the midst of the bleak tundra.
  3. evoke
    call forth, as an emotion, feeling, or response
    She spoke half in Eskimo and half in English, as if the instincts of her father and the science of the gussaks, the white-faced, might evoke some magical combination that would help her get her message through to the wolf.
  4. monotony
    the quality of wearisome constancy and lack of variety
    Not a tree grew anywhere to break the monotony of the gold-green plain, for the soils of the tundra are permanently frozen.
  5. lichen
    a plant occurring in crusty patches on tree trunks or rocks
    Only moss, grass, lichens, and a few hardy flowers take root in the thin upper layer that thaws briefly in summer.
  6. reprimand
    censure severely or angrily
    The reprimanded pup snapped at a crane fly and shook himself.
  7. semaphore
    an apparatus for visual signaling
    Then the black pup flashed his tail like a semaphore signal and they all jumped on Jello again.
  8. undulate
    increase and decrease in volume or pitch
    Jello whined an undulating note and Kapu turned back to the den.
  9. solicit
    request urgently or persistently
    But Jello? Who was he? Where had he come from? Was he a pup of a previous year? Or had he joined the pack just as she had, by soliciting Amaroq for membership in his tribe?
  10. carrion
    the dead and rotting body of an animal; unfit for human food
    The jaeger preyed upon lemmings, small birds, and occasionally carrion.
  11. ravenous
    extremely hungry
    Zing rushed over to the resting Silver and cuddled up against her. He rammed his nose against her teats and taking one in his mouth, ravenously nursed.
  12. wean
    gradually deprive of mother's milk
    The secret of the fat pups was out. They were being weaned from mother’s milk to well-chewed and partially digested food.
  13. lope
    run easily
    He even lifted his tail and head higher than Miyax had ever seen him do, and, acting like the boss wolf, loped up her frost heave.
  14. deference
    a disposition or tendency to yield to the will of others
    “I’m boss,” she said as his tail and head went down in deference to the symbol of leadership.
  15. swath
    a path or strip
    Kapu scratched a wide swath on the ground with his hind feet.
  16. grovel
    show submission or fear
    Rehearsing whimpers and groveling positions as she climbed to her lookout, she got ready to tell Amaroq how helpless she was in his own language.
  17. knoll
    a small natural hill
    Zing charged up the knoll, hit her arm with great force, and knocked her to the ground.
  18. warily
    in a manner marked by keen caution and watchful prudence
    She waved her arm above her head and the threatening pose spoke louder than all her words. Drooping their tails, glancing warily at her, they trotted away—all but Kapu.
  19. conspicuous
    obvious to the eye or mind
    Every so often she tied a bit of red cloth to a clump of grass or around a conspicuous stone. If she was going to hunt in this confusing land, she must leave a trail to lead her home.
  20. gleam
    be shiny, as if wet
    The wind stirred the wolverine trim on Miyax’s hood and the owl turned his gleaming eyes upon her.
  21. acute
    demonstrating ability to recognize or draw fine distinctions
    He turned his head almost upside down to get a more acute focus on her; then unwinding swiftly, he lifted his body and sped off.
  22. viscera
    internal organs collectively
    She plucked the birds, laid them on the ground, and skillfully cut them open with her ulo. Lifting out the warm viscera, she tipped back her head and popped them into her mouth.
  23. apogee
    the farthest point in an orbit around the Earth
    The sun reached its apogee and started down the blue sky of early afternoon.
  24. quell
    overcome or allay
    Breathing deeply to quell a sense of uneasiness, she finally relaxed, unrolled, and sat up.
  25. incorrigible
    impervious to correction by punishment
    Zing, the incorrigible Zing, was nursing again.
  26. stifle
    smother or suppress
    She reached out again, but as she did so, Silver closed her jaws on Miyax’s shoulder and held her immobile. She stifled a scream.
  27. forage
    collect or look around for, as food
    After what seemed like hours of constant foraging she was still hungry—but not starving.
  28. crescendo
    a gradual increase in loudness
    Amaroq howled. Nails barked. Then Amaroq slid into a musical crescendo that Silver joined in. Their voices undulated as each harmonized with the other.
  29. poise
    hold or carry in equilibrium
    The fog closed in briefly, and when it thinned the caribou was poised above Amaroq, his cleaver-like hoofs aimed at his head.
  30. writhe
    move in a twisting or contorted motion
    Nails had a grip on one hind leg. The caribou bucked, writhed, then dropped to his knees.
  31. flail
    thrash about
    His hoofs flailed at the three who were ending the hunt with slashes and blood-letting bites.
  32. scoff
    laugh at with contempt and derision
    Impulsively, she paid tribute to the spirit of the caribou by lifting her arms to the sun. Then, scoffing at herself for being such an old-fashioned Eskimo, she sharpened her man’s knife on a stone and set to work.
  33. pelt
    the dressed hairy coat of a mammal
    Even as she peeled it away from the flesh with her knife, she was surprised how difficult it was to cut and handle, but she worked diligently, for the pelt was almost as valuable as the meat.
  34. deft
    skillful in physical movements; especially of the hands
    With a deft twist of the ulo, she cut off a slice and savored each bite of this, the most nourishing part of the animal.
  35. permafrost
    ground that is permanently frozen
    Every Eskimo family had a deep cellar in the permafrost into which they put game.
