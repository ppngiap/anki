Lewis Carroll Alice's Adventures in Wonderland

Chapters 4–8

Vocabulary.com
Alice falls asleep in a meadow and wakes in a surreal world inhabited by characters such as the White Rabbit, the Red Queen, and the Cheshire Cat. Learn these words from Lewis Carroll's delightful and witty novel.

Explore the Words

definitions & notes only words
  1. ferret
    a small domesticated mammal with a flexible, elongated body
    It was the White Rabbit, trotting slowly back again, and looking anxiously about as it went, as if it had lost something; and she heard it muttering to itself ‘The Duchess! The Duchess! Oh my dear paws! Oh my fur and whiskers! She’ll get me executed, as sure as ferrets are ferrets! Where can I have dropped them, I wonder?’
  2. slate
    thin layers of rock used for roofing
    Here, Bill! catch hold of this rope—Will the roof bear?—Mind that loose slate—Oh, it’s coming down! Heads below!’
  3. feeble
    pathetically lacking in force or effectiveness
    Last came a little feeble, squeaking voice, (‘That’s Bill,’ thought Alice,) ‘Well, I hardly know—No more, thank ye; I’m better now—but I’m a deal too flustered to tell you—all I know is, something comes at me like a Jack-in-the-box, and up I goes like a sky-rocket!’
  4. coax
    influence or urge by gentle urging, caressing, or flattering
    ‘Poor little thing!’ said Alice, in a coaxing tone, and she tried hard to whistle to it; but she was terribly frightened all the time at the thought that it might be hungry, in which case it would be very likely to eat her up in spite of all her coaxing.
  5. languid
    lacking spirit or liveliness
    The Caterpillar and Alice looked at each other for some time in silence: at last the Caterpillar took the hookah out of its mouth, and addressed her in a languid, sleepy voice.
  6. stern
    serious and harsh in manner or behavior
    ‘What do you mean by that?’ said the Caterpillar sternly.
  7. chrysalis
    pupa of a moth or butterfly enclosed in a cocoon
    ‘Well, perhaps you haven’t found it so yet,’ said Alice; ‘but when you have to turn into a chrysalis—you will some day, you know—and then after that into a butterfly, I should think you’ll feel it a little queer, won’t you?’
  8. contemptuously
    without respect; in a disdainful manner
    ‘You!’ said the Caterpillar contemptuously.
  9. incessantly
    without interruption
    ‘You are old, Father William,’ the young man said,
    ‘And your hair has become very white;
    And yet you incessantly stand on your head—
    Do you think, at your age, it is right?’
  10. sage
    a mentor in spiritual and philosophical topics
    ‘In my youth,’ said the sage, as he shook his grey locks,
    ‘I kept all my limbs very supple
    By the use of this ointment—one shilling the box—
    Allow me to sell you a couple?’
  11. supple
    moving and bending with ease
    ‘In my youth,’ said the sage, as he shook his grey locks,
    ‘I kept all my limbs very supple
    By the use of this ointment—one shilling the box—
    Allow me to sell you a couple?’
  12. shilling
    an English coin worth one twentieth of a pound
    ‘In my youth,’ said the sage, as he shook his grey locks,
    ‘I kept all my limbs very supple
    By the use of this ointment—one shilling the box—
    Allow me to sell you a couple?’
  13. piteous
    deserving or inciting a feeling of sympathy and sorrow
    ‘But I’m not used to it!’ pleaded poor Alice in a piteous tone.
  14. morsel
    a small amount of solid food; a mouthful
    Her chin was pressed so closely against her foot, that there was hardly room to open her mouth; but she did it at last, and managed to swallow a morsel of the lefthand bit.
  15. indignantly
    in a manner showing anger at something unjust or wrong
    ‘I’m not a serpent!’ said Alice indignantly.
  16. subdued
    in a softened tone
    ‘Serpent, I say again!’ repeated the Pigeon, but in a more subdued tone, and added with a kind of sob, ‘I’ve tried every way, and nothing seems to suit them!’
  17. livery
    a uniform, especially worn by servants and chauffeurs
    For a minute or two she stood looking at the house, and wondering what to do next, when suddenly a footman in livery came running out of the wood—(she considered him to be a footman because he was in livery: otherwise, judging by his face only, she would have called him a fish)—and rapped loudly at the door with his knuckles.
  18. graze
    break the skin by scraping
    At this moment the door of the house opened, and a large plate came skimming out, straight at the Footman’s head: it just grazed his nose, and broke to pieces against one of the trees behind him.
  19. cauldron
    a very large pot that is used for boiling
    The door led right into a large kitchen, which was full of smoke from one end to the other: the Duchess was sitting on a three-legged stool in the middle, nursing a baby; the cook was leaning over the fire, stirring a large cauldron which seemed to be full of soup.
  20. hearth
    an area near a fireplace extending into a room
    The only things in the kitchen that did not sneeze, were the cook, and a large cat which was sitting on the hearth and grinning from ear to ear.
  21. abide
    put up with something or somebody unpleasant
    ‘Oh, don’t bother me,’ said the Duchess; ‘I never could abide figures!’
  22. bough
    any of the larger branches of a tree
    And she began thinking over other children she knew, who might do very well as pigs, and was just saying to herself, ‘if one only knew the right way to change them—’ when she was a little startled by seeing the Cheshire Cat sitting on a bough of a tree a few yards off.
  23. rave
    talk in a noisy, excited, or declamatory manner
    ‘I’ve seen hatters before,’ she said to herself; ‘the March Hare will be much the most interesting, and perhaps as this is May it won’t be raving mad—at least not so mad as it was in March.’
  24. giddy
    having or causing a whirling sensation; liable to falling
    ‘I said pig,’ replied Alice; ‘and I wish you wouldn’t keep appearing and vanishing so suddenly: you make one quite giddy.’
  25. thatch
    cover with roofing material made of plant stalks
    She had not gone much farther before she came in sight of the house of the March Hare: she thought it must be the right house, because the chimneys were shaped like ears and the roof was thatched with fur.
  26. severity
    excessive sternness
    ‘You should learn not to make personal remarks,’ Alice said with some severity; ‘it’s very rude.’
  27. meek
    evidencing little spirit or courage
    ‘It was the best butter,’ the March Hare meekly replied.
  28. bawl
    shout loudly and without restraint
    ‘Well, I’d hardly finished the first verse,’ said the Hatter, ‘when the Queen jumped up and bawled out, “He’s murdering the time! Off with his head!”’
  29. oblong
    having an elongated form with approximately parallel sides
    First came ten soldiers carrying clubs; these were all shaped like the three gardeners, oblong and flat, with their hands and feet at the corners: next the ten courtiers; these were ornamented all over with diamonds, and walked two and two, as the soldiers did.
  30. crimson
    a deep and vivid red color
    Then followed the Knave of Hearts, carrying the King’s crown on a crimson velvet cushion; and, last of all this grand procession, came THE KING AND QUEEN OF HEARTS.
  31. procession
    the action of a group moving ahead in regular formation
    Then followed the Knave of Hearts, carrying the King’s crown on a crimson velvet cushion; and, last of all this grand procession, came THE KING AND QUEEN OF HEARTS.
  32. furrow
    a long shallow trench in the ground
    Alice thought she had never seen such a curious croquet-ground in her life; it was all ridges and furrows; the balls were live hedgehogs, the mallets live flamingoes, and the soldiers had to double themselves up and to stand on their hands and feet, to make the arches.
  33. provoke
    annoy continually or chronically
    ...it was very provoking to find that the hedgehog had unrolled itself, and was in the act of crawling away...
  34. impertinent
    improperly forward or bold
    ‘Don’t be impertinent,’ said the King, ‘and don’t look at me like that!’
  35. appeal
    request earnestly; ask for aid or protection
    The moment Alice appeared, she was appealed to by all three to settle the question, and they repeated their arguments to her, though, as they all spoke at once, she found it very hard indeed to make out exactly what they said.
