Jean Craighead George Julie of the Wolves

Part III

Vocabulary.com
In this classic novel, an Eskimo girl lost in the arctic wilderness must fight for survival while she grapples with her sense of identity. Learn these words from the Newbery Medal winner by Jean Craighead George.

Explore the Words

definitions & notes only words
  1. brandish
    move or swing back and forth
    Hand tightening on the antler club, brandishing it, growling, she flung herself upon him and bit the top of his nose.
  2. lunge
    make a thrusting forward movement
    Desperate, furious, she lunged at him.
  3. abeyance
    temporary cessation or suspension
    Holding him in abeyance with the antler, she glanced around the ruins of her home and took stock of the damage.
  4. saunter
    walk leisurely and with no apparent aim
    He leapt lightly to the side, snarled, and sauntered away.
  5. gingerly
    in a manner marked by extreme care or delicacy
    Gingerly she snatched the hare, turned, and fled back to camp.
  6. niche
    a small concavity
    She dug a niche in the side of a heave with her man’s knife, folded her ground skin into an envelope, and pushed it into the cave.
  7. lair
    the habitation of wild animals
    Buried in her skins like an animal in its lair, Miyax wondered about Amaroq and Kapu.
  8. deviate
    cause to turn away from a previous or expected course
    She walked a straight line to the ocean, for the pools had frozen and she could follow her compass without deviating.
  9. bravado
    a swaggering show of courage
    Colliding again, they pawed the ground and then peacefully trotted away. Miyax wondered who they were fighting over, for not a female was to be seen. All the bravado and glamour seemed lost on the grass and the sky.
  10. cumbersome
    difficult to handle or use because of size or weight
    Nevertheless, when she had eaten she picked up her pack, tugged on her cumbersome drag, and moved on; for the thundering animals told her she was along the migration path and she did not want to camp in the middle of a caribou love nest.
  11. disquieting
    causing mental discomfort
    The next call, however, was disquieting. The wolf seemed to be saying there was danger in the air.
  12. desolate
    crushed by grief
    Someone had greeted her during the night. It could not have been Jello for the scent did not have the bitter odor of an angry and desolate wolf.
  13. douse
    wet thoroughly
    Furthermore, it was sparsely given, not the dousing given to hostile objects.
  14. larder
    a small storeroom for storing foods or wines
    She marveled at how valuable these simple things were, how beautiful and precious. With them she could make a home, a larder, a sled, and clothes.
  15. feat
    a notable achievement
    By using the plants, animals, and temperature, they had changed the harsh Arctic into a home, a feat as incredible as sending rockets to the moon.
  16. wrest
    obtain by seizing forcibly or violently, also metaphorically
    With a grin she reached in her pocket, found a strip of caribou hide, and thrust it at him. Kapu snatched it, and with one pull not only wrested it from her, but sent her sprawling on her back.
  17. improvise
    perform without preparation
    Then she glided and shifted her weight, gracefully executing a combination of steps which the bent woman had danced long ago at seal camp. When she came to the refrain, however, she did not do the dance about evil spirits, but improvised—as Eskimos do at this point.
  18. caper
    a playful leap or hop
    She told the story of a young wolf who had brought the lost girl a shank of meat, and ended the performance with a Kapu-like caper.
  19. hardy
    able to survive under unfavorable weather conditions
    A willow ptarmigan, the chicken of the tundra, clucked behind her and whistled softly as it hunted seeds. The Arctic Circle had been returned to its permanent bird resident, the hardy ptarmigan.
  20. plaintive
    expressing sorrow
    The millions of voices of summer had died down to one plaintive note.
  21. hibernation
    the resting state in which some animals pass the winter
    Slipping and sliding, she reached the south bank as the grizzly staggered forward, then crumpled to its knees and sat down. She wondered why he was not in hibernation.
  22. veer
    turn sharply; change direction abruptly
    Yapping, barking, darting, the wolves drove the grizzly far out on the tundra. Finally, they veered away and, breaking into a joyous gallop, dashed over the snow and out of sight.
  23. billow
    rise up as if in waves
    The regal pair leaped away, snow billowing up from their strides like smoke.
  24. meander
    move or cause to move in a winding or curving course
    The craft tipped its wings and zigzagged across the sky. When it continued to zag she realized the pilot was following a meandering river where game wintered.
  25. constrict
    squeeze or press together
    Her throat constricted with grief, and sobs choked her.
  26. sprawl
    sit or lie with one's limbs spread out
    Blindly he ran, and as he came by she reached out and tripped him. He sprawled across the snow and lay still.
  27. floe
    a flat mass of ice drifting at sea
    In the Arctic, all artifacts are preserved for ages. Even throwing them into the oceans does not work a change, for the water freezes around them, and in the ice floes they come back on the shores.
  28. totem
    emblem consisting of an object such as an animal or plant
    She took her carving from her pocket, and got down on her knees. Singing softly in Eskimo, she told him she had no bladder for his spirit to dwell in, but that she had his totem. She asked him to enter the totem and be with her forever.
  29. grandeur
    the quality of being magnificent or splendid
    A new grandeur attended his movements as he came through the door and Miyax squeezed his chin.
  30. yearling
    an animal in its second year
    Perhaps Silver and Nails would find a sick yearling and with help from the pups be able to kill it.
  31. wane
    decrease in phase
    Time passed, fountains of the magnetic northern lights came and went, and the moon waxed and waned many times.
  32. temperate
    mild or free from extremes
    And the women never tanned hides anymore; all skins for the tourist trade must go to Seattle to be tanned correctly for the temperate climates where most were shipped.
  33. enamor
    attract
    He had become enamored of hunting and fishing and became so skilled that when his grandfather died he was adopted by the greatest of all living Eskimo hunters.
  34. engulf
    flow over or cover completely
    The woman passed the church and stopped at the mission door. She was engulfed in light for an instant, then the door closed behind her.
  35. resonant
    serving to evoke powerful memories, images, or emotions
    Kapugen’s voice was resonant and warm and seemed to come from the seashore at Nunivak where the birds sang and the sea was framed with the fur of his parka.
